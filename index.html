01 / The Poet

A voice carved from water, wood, and quiet.

Afi is known for raw, unpredictable performances that braid personal experience with sharp social commentary. Her poetic journey is one of devotion — she has given everything to the craft and kept nothing but mastery in return.

A Senior High School graduate, she is also a self-taught fashion designer, and most of the costumes she wears on stage come from her own hands and her own thread. The performance, the garment, the verse — they arrive as one piece of work.

She loves anything tied to a body of water. She loves the woods. She moves through both with a childlike energy that the stage never quite tames. Music is her love. Animated films drive her. And solitude — being quiet, being alone — is her superpower.

She is a whole force. A poet who has given her hours, her quiet, and her hands to the craft — and she walks away from each stage with the audience still trying to find their breath.

04 / The Atelier

She sews her own armour.

Most of what Afi wears on stage, she has cut and stitched herself. The poem and the garment are written in the same hand. There is no costume designer between her and the audience — only thread, only intention.

It is a quiet kind of audacity: a young woman making the verse, the dress, the moment, all from one chair, one needle, one mind that refuses to wait.
